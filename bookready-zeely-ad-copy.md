# BookReady — Zeely.ai ad copy source document

Longer descriptions and target audience paragraphs for AI generation.
Each entry is structured the same way so you can paste straight into
Zeely's product fields:

- **Name** — the headline / product name
- **Description** — what it is, who it's for, what it replaces, why it
  wins. Written long so the AI has plenty to riff on for hooks, image
  briefs, and short-form scripts.
- **Target audience** — the buyer in detail: role, age, income context,
  where they hang out, what they currently use, what they're frustrated
  by, and how they make purchase decisions.

## 1. BookReady — master brand

**Name:** BookReady

**Description:**
BookReady is booking software built specifically for beauty professionals
who are tired of looking like every other beauty business. Where most
booking tools force you into a generic template that screams "booking
form," BookReady wraps a real customer-facing website around your
calendar so your booking page actually looks like your work.

Each business gets a fully branded site at `{their-name}.bkrdy.me` (or
their own custom domain on the higher tiers) with nine template skins
spanning every corner of beauty: barbers, lash artists, nail techs,
facialists, hairstylists, brow and PMU artists, estheticians, tattoo
artists, and medspas. Everything connects: a public site with portfolio,
services, and policies that pulls into one cohesive customer flow,
booking with deposits via Stripe Connect, customer accounts that
remember preferences, automatic appointment reminders by email and SMS,
a waitlist that fills cancellations, capacity-aware availability that
actually respects how you work, and a clean dashboard that tells you who
your top spenders are and where the holes in your week are.

No setup fees, no "professional services" upcharges, no five-year
contracts. Plans start at $24 a month with a 14-day free trial. You
bring the brand, BookReady brings the booking.

**Target audience:**
Independent beauty professionals and small studio owners between 22 and
45, predominantly female, Instagram and TikTok native. They run their
business out of a single chair, a small studio with two to five
chairs, or a salon with up to twenty-five staff. Almost all of them
got started by posting their work on Instagram and built a clientele
through DMs before realizing they needed a real booking system.

They currently stitch together free tools (Linktree, Calendly, Square
Appointments) or pay too much for legacy beauty software (GlossGenius,
Vagaro, Booksy, Mindbody, Phorest, Boulevard) that has every feature
under the sun but looks like it was designed in 2011. They hate having
their booking page look like a Squarespace template, hate the way

and-dimes for every add-on, and hate Mindbody's enterprise pricing for
a four-chair salon.

They buy on aesthetics, recommendation from peers, and the promise of
being able to set everything up themselves in an evening without
calling a support rep. They almost never read feature lists in full.
They click on Instagram ads that show the actual booking page, screen
share with a friend who already uses it, and sign up if the trial
doesn't require a credit card up front. Average decision time from
discovery to sign-up: two to four weeks.

They buy based on what they see on TikTok, what other solo pros in

## Notes on using this in Zeely

For each Zeely product, paste the Name into the product name field,
the Description into the product description field, and the Target
audience into the audience field. Zeely's AI will use those paragraphs
to generate ad copy, image briefs, and video scripts.

A few tactical notes:

1. **The Name field is short on purpose.** Don't shorten it further or
   Zeely will optimize for keyword density and lose the brand. "BookReady
   Solo" reads better than "Solo Plan."

2. **The Description should stay long.** Zeely's AI generates better
   ad variants when it has 200+ words to riff on, especially for
   image-and-script ad formats.

3. **Target audience paragraphs deliberately include competitor names
   (Square Appointments, GlossGenius, Mindbody, etc.).** This helps
   Zeely surface "switch from X" angles automatically when generating
   competitive ads.

4. **The audience age + gender split is intentional.** Beauty industry
   demographics are real and ignoring them produces generic ads. If
   Zeely flags an angle as "too gendered" you can soften the audience
   paragraph for that one product, but in the original copy each
   target was tuned to the actual market.

5. **For templates specifically, the description is also a brief.**
   When Zeely generates images for a template ad, the description
   gives the AI a clear visual direction (color, mood, typography).
   Don't strip the visual cues out.
